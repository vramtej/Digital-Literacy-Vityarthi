## Stay Safe Online: Safety Tips for College Students

## Financial and UPI Safety
Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required when you are sending money through apps like Google Pay or PhonePe.
Before making any payment, check the UPI ID and the account holder’s name shown on the screen. A small mistake can send money to the wrong person.
Never share your OTP, bank details, passwords, or UPI PIN with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank, college, or customer care service.

## Account and Password Security
Use strong passwords for your accounts. A good password should include letters, numbers, and symbols.
Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for important accounts such as email, social media, and banking apps. This gives extra protection if your password is stolen.
Do not use the same password for every account. If one account gets hacked, the others may also become unsafe.

## Safe Internet Usage
Avoid using public Wi-Fi in places such as cafes, railway stations, or college common areas for banking or online payments. Use your mobile data or a trusted network instead.
Keep your phone, laptop, and apps updated regularly. Updates fix security problems and protect your device from new threats.
Download apps only from trusted sources such as the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Unofficial apps may contain harmful software.

## Protection from Online Scams
Be careful with messages, emails, or calls that create panic or urgency. Scammers often say things like “Your account will be blocked” or “Click immediately.”
Do not open unknown links or attachments sent through email, SMS, or apps like WhatsApp unless you are sure they are safe.
If an offer looks too good to be true, such as free prizes, easy money, or huge discounts, it is probably a scam.

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately. Quick action increases the chance of stopping the fraud.

Report online at National Cyber Crime Portal
Call the National Cybercrime Helpline at 1930 anytime, day or night.
